Scalping

Category: By Trade Duration

Scalping is a short-term trading strategy used by traders (called scalpers) to profit from small price movements in financial markets such as forex, stocks, crypto, or futures. The goal is to make many quick trades throughout the day, capturing tiny profits that can add up to a significant amount by the end of a trading session.

üß† Basic Concept

Scalping is based on the idea that:

‚ÄúSmall moves happen more frequently than large moves.‚Äù

Instead of waiting hours or days for big price swings, scalpers focus on minute-to-minute fluctuations. Each trade may last only a few seconds to a few minutes, and profits per trade are small ‚Äî often just a few cents or pips.

‚öôÔ∏è How Scalping Works

Fast Entry and Exit: Traders open and close positions quickly to capture tiny gains. (Example: Buying a stock at $100.00 and selling at $100.05 for a 5¬¢ profit.)

High Trade Frequency: Scalpers might execute dozens or even hundreds of trades per day.

Small Targets and Tight Stops: Take-profit target is small (e.g., 5‚Äì10 pips in forex), and the stop-loss is also small to limit losses.

High Leverage (sometimes): Many scalpers use leverage to magnify small price movements ‚Äî but this also increases risk.

Technical Analysis Focused: Scalping relies heavily on technical indicators, chart patterns, and price action, not on fundamental analysis.

üìä Common Tools & Indicators

  • Moving Averages (MA): To identify short-term trends.
  • Bollinger Bands: To detect volatility and breakout opportunities.
  • RSI or Stochastic Oscillator: For overbought/oversold signals.
  • Volume Indicators: To confirm trade strength.
  • Level II Quotes / Order Book: To see real-time bid and ask prices.

üíº Example of a Scalping Trade

Let‚Äôs say a trader is scalping EUR/USD in forex:

  • Buys at: 1.1000
  • Sets a take profit at: 1.1003 (3 pips gain)
  • Sets a stop loss at: 1.0998 (2 pips loss)

If successful, the trader gains 3 pips. This process might be repeated 50‚Äì100 times a day.

‚öñÔ∏è Advantages of Scalping

  • ‚úÖ Frequent Opportunities: Markets move constantly, offering many setups.
  • ‚úÖ Low Exposure Time: Reduced risk from sudden market reversals.
  • ‚úÖ Quick Feedback: You know fast whether your strategy works.
  • ‚úÖ Can Compound Profits: Small but frequent wins can add up.

‚ö†Ô∏è Disadvantages of Scalping

  • ‚ùå High Transaction Costs: Commissions and spreads can eat profits.
  • ‚ùå Requires Intense Focus: Constant monitoring and fast reactions needed.
  • ‚ùå Emotionally Demanding: Many small trades can cause stress and fatigue.
  • ‚ùå Not Ideal for Beginners: Requires strong discipline and experience.
  • ‚ùå Dependence on Speed: Professional scalpers often use low-latency trading platforms.

üïí Typical Timeframes

Market Common Scalping Chart Timeframes
Forex 1-minute, 5-minute charts
Stocks Tick charts, 1-minute charts
Crypto 15-second, 1-minute charts

üß© Scalping Strategies

Market Making: Placing simultaneous buy and sell limit orders around the current price to profit from spreads.

Momentum Scalping: Trading during high volatility when price breaks out of a range.

Range Scalping: Buying at support and selling at resistance within a tight range.

News Scalping: Entering trades during economic announcements with fast-moving prices.

üìà Who Uses Scalping?

  • Day traders seeking consistent daily profits.
  • Algorithmic and high-frequency traders (HFTs).
  • Institutional traders with access to ultra-fast execution.
  • Retail traders who can manage costs and execute quickly.

üßÆ Example Profit Calculation

Suppose:

  • You make 50 trades/day.
  • Average profit per trade = $10.
  • Total daily profit = $500.
  • If you lose on 10 trades (-$10 each), net profit = $400.

Even with small profits per trade, high frequency can yield solid returns.

üèÅ Conclusion

Scalping is a precision-based, fast-paced trading style best suited for traders who:

  • Can make quick decisions under pressure,
  • Have reliable technology (low spreads, fast execution),
  • And possess the discipline to stick to strict risk management.

While it can be profitable, it‚Äôs also one of the most challenging trading strategies due to its speed, cost, and psychological demands.

Day Trading

Category: By Trade Duration

Day trading (ya Intraday trading) mein traders ek hi trading day ke andar securities ko buy aur sell karte hain. Market close hone se pehle sabhi positions ko square off kar diya jaata hai, taaki overnight risk (jaise ki market gap-up ya gap-down open hona) se bacha ja sake. Yeh scalping se thoda lamba, par swing trading se chota hota hai.

Swing Trading

Category: By Trade Duration

Swing trading mein positions ko kuch dino se lekar kuch hafton tak hold kiya jaata hai. Iska aim short-term price "swings" ya market trends se profit kamaana hota hai. Swing traders aksar technical analysis (jaise chart patterns aur indicators) ka istemaal karke entry aur exit points dhoondhte hain.

Position Trading

Category: By Trade Duration

Position trading sabse longest-term trading strategy hai, jo investing ke kaafi kareeb hai. Ismein traders positions ko mahino ya saalon tak hold karte hain, taaki long-term market trends se fayda utha sakein. Ismein daily price fluctuations ko ignore kiya jaata hai aur fundamental analysis par zyaada dhyaan diya jaata hai.

By Strategy

Momentum Trading

Category: By Strategy

Momentum trading is strategy hai jo "trend is your friend" ke principle par kaam karti hai. Traders un stocks ko khareedte hain jo pehle se hi upar jaa rahe hain (strong momentum) aur unko bechte hain jo neeche gir rahe hain. Iska idea yeh hai ki jo trend chal raha hai, woh kuch waqt tak continue rahega.

Fundamental Trading

Category: By Strategy

Fundamental trading mein kisi asset ki intrinsic value (asli keemat) ka pata lagaya jaata hai. Iske liye company ki financial statements (jaise balance sheet, P&L statement), industry trends, economic data, aur management quality ko analyze kiya jaata hai. Agar stock price apni intrinsic value se kam hai, toh use "undervalued" maankar khareeda jaata hai.

Technical Trading

Category: By Strategy

Technical trading (Technical Analysis par based) mein traders price charts aur trading volume ka istemaal karke future price movements ko predict karte hain. Ismein maana jaata hai ki saari information (jaise news, fundamentals) pehle se hi price mein reflected hoti hai. Traders chart patterns, trendlines, aur indicators (jaise RSI, MACD) ka istemaal karte hain.

Arbitrage Trading

Category: By Strategy

Arbitrage ek low-risk strategy hai jisme trader ek hi asset ko do alag-alag markets mein ek saath khareedta aur bechta hai. Aisa tab kiya jaata hai jab asset ki keemat dono markets mein alag-alag ho. Maqsad is price difference se risk-free profit kamaana hota hai. For example, ek stock ko NSE par kam daam mein khareedna aur usi waqt BSE par zyaada daam mein bech dena.

By Methodology

Algorithmic Trading

Category: By Methodology

Algorithmic trading (ya Algo-trading) mein computer programs ka istemaal karke trades ko automatically execute kiya jaata hai. Yeh programs pehle se define kiye gaye rules (jaise price, timing, volume) par kaam karte hain. Iska fayda speed (insaanon se tez) aur accuracy (bina emotions ke) hai. High-Frequency Trading (HFT) iska ek advanced form hai.

Social Trading

Category: By Methodology

Social trading ek naya concept hai jisme traders (khaaskar naye) doosre expert traders ke trading behavior ko dekhte hain aur unse seekhte hain. Yeh social media jaisa hota hai, lekin trading ke liye. Traders apne ideas share karte hain, market analysis post karte hain aur doosron ki strategies ko follow kar sakte hain.

Copy Trading

Category: By Methodology

Copy trading, social trading ka ek hissa hai jisme aap kisi doosre successful trader ki trades ko "copy" karte hain. Jab woh expert trader koi trade leta hai (buy ya sell), toh wahi trade aapke account mein bhi automatically execute ho jaati hai. Yeh naye traders ke liye popular hai jo experts ki strategy se fayda uthaana chahte hain.

News Trading

Category: By Methodology

News trading mein traders economic data releases (jaise GDP, inflation, unemployment data) ya company-specific news (jaise earnings results, merger announcements) ke aate hi turant trade lete hain. Iska aim news ke kaaran hone waale sudden aur strong price movements se profit kamaana hota hai. Ismein high volatility aur risk hota hai.

Delivery Trading

Category: By Methodology

Delivery trading mein aap stocks ko khareedte hain aur unhe apne Demat account mein "deliver" lete hain. Ismein aap stocks ko ek din se zyaada (hafton, mahino, ya saalon tak) hold kar sakte hain. Yeh intraday trading ka ulta hai. Ismein aapko poora paisa dena hota hai (koi margin nahi milta) aur aap stocks ke legal owner ban jaate hain.

Other Trading Types

Commodity Trading

Category: Other Types

Commodity trading mein raw materials (commodities) ko khareeda aur becha jaata hai. Yeh do tarah ke hote hain: Hard commodities (jaise Gold, Silver, Crude Oil) aur Soft commodities (jaise Wheat, Cotton, Sugar). Traders inke future price movements par speculation karte hain. Yeh aksar futures contracts ke through kiya jaata hai.

Options Trading

Category: Other Types

Options trading derivatives ka ek form hai. Ek 'Option' ek contract hota hai jo buyer ko 'right' (haq) deta hai, lekin 'obligation' (majboori) nahi, ki woh ek asset ko ek specific price (strike price) par ek specific date (expiry date) tak khareed (Call Option) ya bech (Put Option) sake. Iska istemaal speculation aur hedging (risk kam karne) dono ke liye hota hai.

Cryptocurrency Trading

Category: Other Types

Cryptocurrency trading mein digital assets jaise Bitcoin, Ethereum, etc. ko khareeda aur becha jaata hai. Yeh market 24/7 chalta hai aur bahut zyaada volatile (asteer) hota hai. Traders in crypto assets ki keemat ke utaar-chadhaav par speculation karke profit kamaane ki koshish karte hain.

Futures Trading

Category: Other Types

Futures bhi derivatives hain. Yeh ek legal contract hota hai jisme aap ek asset (jaise commodity, stock, ya currency) ko future ki ek specific date par ek pehle se tay (pre-determined) price par khareedne ya bechne ke liye agree karte hain. Options ke ‡§µ‡§ø‡§™‡§∞‡•Ä‡§§, Futures mein 'obligation' (majboori) hoti hai, 'right' nahi.

Price Action Trading

Category: Other Types

Price action trading technical analysis ka ek form hai jisme traders sirf "price" ke movement par dhyaan dete hain. Ismein lagging indicators (jaise RSI, MACD) ka istemaal nahi kiya jaata. Traders candlestick patterns, support & resistance levels, aur trend lines ko padhkar trading decisions lete hain.
